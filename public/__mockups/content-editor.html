If you’re looking at Bridgeland and wondering what $850k actually buys you in 2026, this video is the answer.

[MID-VIDEO HOOK] I’m going to show you three real listings from the last month, what they sold for, how long they sat, and what the trade-offs were. By the end you’ll know whether Bridgeland fits your budget, or whether you should be looking at Renfrew or Sunnyside instead.

[SIDEWAYS CREDIBILITY] A bit of context before I get into it. I’ve been working Calgary’s inner city for fourteen years, and Bridgeland specifically since the Centre Street BRT plans first dropped in 2018. So the data I’m about to walk through, I’ve pulled directly from my brokerage’s MLS feed for March 2026.

What the numbers actually say.

The median detached sale price in Bridgeland for March was [STAT]$852,000. That’s up six percent year over year. The average property in that price band sold for [STAT]4.14% over list price, and the median days on market was [STAT]eighteen days. So when I say the market is active, I mean it.

But the headline number hides a lot. There’s a huge range underneath $850k, and the trade-offs matter more than the average.

[LEAD MAGNET 1/3] If you want the full breakdown by street, lot size, and finish quality, I’ve put it all in my Bridgeland Buyer’s Guide. Link in the description, takes about three minutes to flip through.

Listing one: the bungalow on 7th.

First property, classic 1950s bungalow on a 50-foot lot just off Centre Street. Listed at $799k, sold for $832k after eleven days. So roughly four percent over list, right in line with the market.

What you get: 1,100 square feet on the main, a finished basement, two parking off the back lane, and one of the deepest yards I’ve seen in the neighbourhood. The kitchen is original but functional. Bathrooms are dated. So you’re buying location and land, with a renovation runway whenever you’re ready.

Listing two: the new infill on 6A.

Opposite end of the spectrum. Two-storey infill, 2,100 square feet above grade, triple-pane windows, heated garage, ten-foot ceilings on the main. Listed at $899k, sold for $878k after twenty-three days.

So we’re actually looking at a place that sold a bit under list, which is the first hint that buyers in this band are getting more selective. [SIDEWAYS CREDIBILITY] I’ve had two of my own clients look at infills like this in the last six weeks, and both walked away because the lot was too narrow for a future garden suite.

[LEAD MAGNET 2/3] If you’re trying to figure out whether a Bridgeland infill is the right call for you specifically, I’ve built a 10-question fit quiz. Second link in the description, takes ninety seconds.

So, can you live in Bridgeland for $850k?

Yes. With trade-offs. Renovation runway on a bungalow, narrower lot on a newer infill, or a heritage home that’ll cap your future flexibility. Pick the one whose compromises you can live with.

[LEAD MAGNET 3/3] If you want me to send you the three listings I think are the closest match to what you’re looking for, just message me on Instagram, handle’s in the description.
