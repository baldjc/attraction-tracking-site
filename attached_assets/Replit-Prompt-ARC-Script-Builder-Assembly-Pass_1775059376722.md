# Replit Prompt — ARC Script Builder: Assembly Pass (Curiosity Bridges, Stories, Lead Magnet 3x, Grade 5 Language)

## IMPORTANT: Do NOT change the model string. It must remain exactly as-is.

## What to change

After all 7 sections of the script are individually approved and the full script is assembled, add a single **Assembly Pass** step before the script is marked complete. This pass reviews the full script end-to-end and handles four things in sequence.

---

### Step 1: Story / Scenario Prompt (one time only)

After assembling the full script, check whether any of the three Insight sections contain a real client story, relatable scenario, or grounding example. If any insight sections are missing one, show the member a single prompt:

> "Can you think of any real client stories that could work in this video? A specific situation, outcome, or conversation that relates to the insights you're teaching?"

Give the member two clear options:
1. **Yes** — the member provides a story and the AI suggests where to place it in the strongest insight section, then rewrites that section to include it
2. **No, use hypothetical examples instead** — the AI generates relatable hypothetical scenarios (using "Imagine if..." or "Picture this..." framing) based on the member's avatar profile data and places them in the insight sections that need grounding

The member approves the updated insight sections before moving on.

---

### Step 2: Curiosity Bridge Transitions

Review the complete script end-to-end and rewrite every major section transition using And/But/Therefore curiosity bridges. These are the transitions that pull the viewer from one section to the next without dropping off.

Show the member a before/after comparison of each transition. The member approves the final set of transitions.

---

### Step 3: Lead Magnet 3x Placement Check

Verify the lead magnet appears in all three required placements:

1. **Opening** — within the first 15-20 seconds, right after the intro pattern
2. **Mid-roll** — at approximately the 2/3 mark of the script, tied to a curiosity bridge
3. **Closing** — paired with the binge architecture / next-video push

If any of the three placements are missing, the AI automatically writes the missing placement(s) and presents them to the member. The member can accept, edit, or provide their own version.

---

### Step 4: Grade 5 Language Scan

Scan the complete assembled script for:
- Real estate jargon or industry-specific terms that a non-agent viewer wouldn't immediately understand
- Complex sentence structures that could be simplified
- Any language that sounds like a listing description or corporate communication rather than a peer-to-peer conversation

For each flagged item, suggest a simpler alternative. The member can accept or reject each suggestion individually.

---

### Flow summary

After this Assembly Pass, the final script is marked complete. The full builder flow becomes:

1. Member works through 7 sections (existing flow — do not change)
2. Each section approved individually (existing flow — do not change)
3. Full script assembled (existing flow — do not change)
4. **Assembly Pass begins:**
   - 4a. Story/scenario prompt (one time, two paths)
   - 4b. Curiosity bridge transitions (before/after shown)
   - 4c. Lead magnet 3x placement check
   - 4d. Grade 5 language scan
5. Member approves final assembled script
6. Script marked complete

## Why this matters

Sections that are strong individually often feel abrupt or disconnected when stitched together. The assembly pass ensures the final script has emotional grounding (stories), smooth transitions (curiosity bridges), proper lead generation structure (3x lead magnet), and accessible language (Grade 5 check) — all core ARC framework principles that separate a good script from a great one.
