# Replit Prompt: Consistency Recalibration + Show Don't Tell Weight Removal

> **Date:** March 15, 2026
> **What this fixes:** Two scoring issues found during monthly audit testing

---

## Fix 1: Consistency Scoring — Must Be Calculated Mathematically

### The Problem
The AI is vibes-scoring Consistency instead of calculating it from upload dates. A weekly publisher got scored 3.0 on a monthly audit (should be 10). The monthly audit window has fewer videos (3–4 vs 5), and the AI is misjudging cadence from the smaller sample.

### What to Change

**In the scoring prompt sent to Claude (wherever the 16 principles rubric is injected)**, replace the Consistency rubric with:

```
16. Consistency (0–10)

What to look for: Publishing cadence and regularity. Weekly is the gold standard.
You MUST calculate this mathematically from upload dates — do not guess.

How to calculate: Take the upload dates of all videos analysed, calculate the average
gap in days between consecutive uploads, then apply this scale:

| Avg Gap (days) | Score |
|----------------|-------|
| ≤7 (weekly or more) | 10 |
| 8–10 | 8 |
| 11–14 (biweekly) | 5 |
| 15–21 | 3 |
| 22–30 | 2 |
| 31+ | 1 |

For monthly audits with fewer than 5 videos: If only 3–4 videos fall in the audit
window, that's expected for a weekly publisher in a 4-week period. Calculate the gap
from the available videos. 4 videos in 4 weeks = weekly = 10.
3 videos in 4 weeks ≈ 9-day gap = 8.

Evidence to cite: List all upload dates. State the calculated average gap in days. Show the math.
```

**In the scoring prompt SCORING RULES section**, add this line:
```
- For Consistency: you MUST calculate the average gap between upload dates in days and use the lookup table. Do not estimate cadence — do the math.
```

## Fix 2: Show Don't Tell — Remove from Weighted Score

### The Problem
Show Don't Tell evaluates visual elements (B-roll, screen shares, maps, iPad diagrams), but the system analyses transcripts, not video footage. The AI has no way to see what's on screen — it can only detect verbal cues like "as you can see on this chart." Scores swing wildly between audits because the AI is guessing. A channel got 8.0 on baseline and 4.0 on monthly for the same visual style.

### What to Change

**1. Update the Show Don't Tell rubric in the scoring prompt:**

```
10. Show Don't Tell (0–10) — TRANSCRIPT-ESTIMATED, NOT INCLUDED IN WEIGHTED SCORE

Important: This principle is scored based on verbal cues in the transcript only.
Since you are analysing transcripts, not video footage, you cannot see visual elements.
Score based ONLY on verbal references to visuals in the transcript.

What to look for (in transcript): Verbal references to visual elements — mentions of
charts, maps, screen shares, B-roll, walkthroughs, iPad drawings, overlays, diagrams.
Phrases like "as you can see," "look at this," "here's what that looks like,"
"let me show you," "on screen right now."

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 0–2 | No verbal references to any visual elements in transcript. |
| 3–4 | Rare verbal references to visuals (1–2 mentions across all videos). |
| 5–6 | Some verbal references but inconsistent. |
| 7–8 | Regular verbal references to visual elements throughout transcripts. |
| 9–10 | Abundant verbal cues to visuals throughout all transcripts. |

Evidence to cite: Quote specific verbal cues that reference visual elements.
Note: this is an estimate from transcript language only.
```

**2. Remove Show Don't Tell from the weighted score calculation.**

Currently the weighted score formula is:
- 3x: Lead Magnet System, Avatar Clarity, Binge Architecture
- 2x: ARC Attention, Approve the Click, Connection Language, Title Frameworks, ARC Revelation, Story Proof
- 1x: Themes Over Topics, Consistency, **Show Don't Tell**, Curiosity Bridges, Values Peppering, Grade 5 Language, ARC Connection
- Denominator: **28**

Change to:
- 3x: Lead Magnet System, Avatar Clarity, Binge Architecture (= 9)
- 2x: ARC Attention, Approve the Click, Connection Language, Title Frameworks, ARC Revelation, Story Proof (= 12)
- 1x: Themes Over Topics, Consistency, Curiosity Bridges, Values Peppering, Grade 5 Language, ARC Connection (= 6)
- **Show Don't Tell: 0x weight** (still scored, still displayed on report, just not in the calculation)
- Denominator: **27**

**3. Update Content Impact dimension** (used in lead audit reports):

Old: `Content Impact = average(ARC Attention, ARC Revelation, Approve the Click, Title Frameworks, Show Don't Tell, Curiosity Bridges)` — divided by 6

New: `Content Impact = average(ARC Attention, ARC Revelation, Approve the Click, Title Frameworks, Curiosity Bridges)` — divided by 5

**4. On the report UI**, Show Don't Tell should still appear in the 16-Principle Scorecard but with a small note or icon indicating it's transcript-estimated and not included in the overall score. Something like a small "(est.)" or tooltip.

## Fix 3: Opening (ARC Attention) Scoring — Stop Inventing Stricter Timing Rules

### The Problem
The AI is inventing timing rules that don't exist. On a single video audit, it said "should lead within 5 seconds" (there is no 5-second rule) and penalised a hook that lands at :15 as "needs to come faster." The video had all elements (hook, title confirmation, lead magnet, content start) landing by :25 — that's a textbook perfect opening — but it scored 6.5 instead of 9–10.

### What to Change

**In the scoring prompt, add these calibration rules after the ARC Attention rubric:**

```
OPENING CALIBRATION RULES (critical — do not invent stricter timing than this):

- Lead magnet mentioned within first 20 seconds = perfect (9–10 for timing)
- Hook landing within 15–20 seconds = excellent — do NOT require hooks to land
  in 5 seconds. There is NO "5 second rule."
- Into revelation content by :25–30 = textbook perfect opening — score 9–10
- "What most people get wrong" is a strong problem hook pattern — do not suggest
  it needs to come faster if it lands naturally within 15–20 seconds
- Score ARC Attention 9–10 when ALL elements are present and land by :30
  (hook creates tension/curiosity, title promise confirmed, lead magnet mentioned,
  content has begun)
- The opening window is 20–30 seconds for a complete sequence. Never penalise
  an opening for imagined timing issues when the structure is working.
```

**Also add to the single video audit's Opening phase prompt (the "Video Phase Analysis" section):**

```
When scoring the Opening phase, check: are all elements present and landing by :30?
If yes, this is a 9–10 opening regardless of whether individual elements land at
:05 or :15. A well-structured problem hook that takes 15 seconds to set up is NOT
"too slow" — it's doing its job. Only penalise openings where elements are MISSING
or land AFTER :30.
```

## Fix 4: Title Frameworks — Titles Should NOT Give Away the Insight

### The Problem
The AI is penalising titles for "not being specific enough about the unique insight." This is backwards. In Attraction by Video methodology, the title creates a **curiosity gap** — the unique insight is the revelation delivered inside the video. Giving away the insight in the title kills the reason to click. A title using a proven framework pattern that creates curiosity was scored 5.5 when it should be 8+.

### What to Change

**In the scoring prompt, add these calibration rules after the Title Frameworks rubric:**

```
TITLE CALIBRATION RULES:

- Titles should create curiosity, NOT give away the unique insight. The insight is
  the revelation delivered inside the video. A title that gives away the answer
  kills the reason to click.
  GOOD: "Why Calgary's Market Is About to Shift" (creates curiosity)
  BAD: "Calgary's Market Is Shifting Because of X Policy" (gave away the answer)
- A title using a proven framework pattern (Why [Audience] Are Getting [Negative
  Outcome], [Number] Mistakes, Don't [Action] Until You [Action], etc.) that
  creates a curiosity gap is doing its job well. Do NOT penalise it for "not
  being specific enough about the insight."
- Score the title on these 4 criteria:
  1. Does it use a proven framework?
  2. Does it create a curiosity gap?
  3. Does it target the right audience?
  4. Would it stop a scroll?
  If yes to all four = 8–10.
```

## Fix 5: Story Proof — Vague Details Are Correct in Real Estate

### The Problem
The AI is penalising client stories for being "vague" on details. But in real estate, agents **cannot** share personal client information — names, addresses, exact prices are confidential. A story like "a family who sold fast and couldn't find a home in time, leading to a double move" is exactly how you're supposed to tell client stories. The AI scored this 6.0 when the presence of a story with narrative structure should be 7–8.

### What to Change

**Replace the Story Proof rubric in the scoring prompt with:**

```
13. Story Proof (0–10)

What to look for: Client stories that prove credibility through real examples.
Stories should have a situation, a challenge, and an outcome.

IMPORTANT: In real estate, agents cannot share personal client details (names,
addresses, exact prices) due to confidentiality. Stories SHOULD be vague on
personal details — that's professional, not a weakness. Score based on whether
stories are PRESENT and have narrative structure, not on how much detail is shared.

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 0–2 | Zero client stories. No social proof. All theory, no evidence. |
| 3–4 | Only generic references with no narrative — "I've worked with people who..." |
| 5–6 | One story with some structure across multiple videos, or multiple vague mentions without a clear arc. |
| 7–8 | Client stories with clear narrative (situation → challenge → outcome) even if details are anonymised. |
| 9–10 | Rich storytelling. Multiple proof stories per video, each with a clear arc. |

CALIBRATION:
- A story with anonymised details but clear narrative structure = 7–8, not 5–6
- "A family who sold fast and couldn't find a home in time" = strong story proof (7–8)
- Only score 3–4 when there's no actual story — just vague hand-waving like
  "my clients love this area"
```

## Fix 6: Single Video Audits — Exclude Consistency, Reframe Themes Over Topics

### The Problem
Consistency (publishing cadence) is a channel-level metric that can't be assessed from a single video. The AI acknowledges this ("consistency assessed at channel level") but still scores it and lets it drag down the single video score. Meanwhile, Themes Over Topics needs to be reframed for single video context — it should check whether the video speaks to the member's avatar, not whether the channel has multiple themes.

### What to Change

**1. Exclude Consistency from single video audits entirely:**
- Do NOT score Consistency on single video audits — mark as "N/A — channel-level metric"
- Remove it from the single video scorecard row (or show as N/A greyed out)
- Calculate the Video Attraction Score by dividing by **15** instead of 16
- In the Video Phase Analysis → Channel Strategy section, show "Consistency — N/A (channel-level metric)"

**2. Reframe Themes Over Topics for single video context:**

In the single video audit scoring prompt, add:
```
For Themes Over Topics in single video audits: Score based on avatar alignment —
is this video clearly speaking to the member's avatar throughout?

Pull the member's saved avatar profile and include it in the prompt context.
If no avatar is saved, reference their most recent baseline or monthly audit.

Scoring scale for single video:
- 10 = Video clearly speaks to the avatar and talks to that person all the way through
- 7–8 = Mostly on-avatar but drifts slightly or addresses a secondary audience briefly
- 5–6 = Tries to talk to a few different people, diluting the avatar focus
- 3–4 = Loosely related to the avatar but not clearly speaking to them
- 0–2 = Not speaking to the avatar at all — wrong audience entirely
```

When running a single video audit, the system must pull the member's avatar profile from their record and pass it into the scoring prompt so the AI can assess alignment.

**3. Update the scorecard display:**
- Consistency row should show "N/A" with greyed-out styling instead of a score
- The Δ column for Consistency should also show "N/A" or be blank
