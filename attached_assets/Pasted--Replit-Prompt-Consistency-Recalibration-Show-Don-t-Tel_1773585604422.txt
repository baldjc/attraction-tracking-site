# Replit Prompt: Consistency Recalibration + Show Don't Tell Weight Removal

> **Date:** March 15, 2026
> **What this fixes:** Two scoring issues found during monthly audit testing

---

## Fix 1: Consistency Scoring — Must Be Calculated Mathematically

### The Problem
The AI is vibes-scoring Consistency instead of calculating it from upload dates. A weekly publisher got scored 3.0 on a monthly audit (should be 10). The monthly audit window has fewer videos (3–4 vs 5), and the AI is misjudging cadence from the smaller sample.

### What to Change

**In the scoring prompt sent to Claude (wherever the 16 principles rubric is injected)**, replace the Consistency rubric with:

```
16. Consistency (0–10)

What to look for: Publishing cadence and regularity. Weekly is the gold standard.
You MUST calculate this mathematically from upload dates — do not guess.

How to calculate: Take the upload dates of all videos analysed, calculate the average
gap in days between consecutive uploads, then apply this scale:

| Avg Gap (days) | Score |
|----------------|-------|
| ≤7 (weekly or more) | 10 |
| 8–10 | 8 |
| 11–14 (biweekly) | 5 |
| 15–21 | 3 |
| 22–30 | 2 |
| 31+ | 1 |

For monthly audits with fewer than 5 videos: If only 3–4 videos fall in the audit
window, that's expected for a weekly publisher in a 4-week period. Calculate the gap
from the available videos. 4 videos in 4 weeks = weekly = 10.
3 videos in 4 weeks ≈ 9-day gap = 8.

Evidence to cite: List all upload dates. State the calculated average gap in days. Show the math.
```

**In the scoring prompt SCORING RULES section**, add this line:
```
- For Consistency: you MUST calculate the average gap between upload dates in days and use the lookup table. Do not estimate cadence — do the math.
```

## Fix 2: Show Don't Tell — Remove from Weighted Score

### The Problem
Show Don't Tell evaluates visual elements (B-roll, screen shares, maps, iPad diagrams), but the system analyses transcripts, not video footage. The AI has no way to see what's on screen — it can only detect verbal cues like "as you can see on this chart." Scores swing wildly between audits because the AI is guessing. A channel got 8.0 on baseline and 4.0 on monthly for the same visual style.

### What to Change

**1. Update the Show Don't Tell rubric in the scoring prompt:**

```
10. Show Don't Tell (0–10) — TRANSCRIPT-ESTIMATED, NOT INCLUDED IN WEIGHTED SCORE

Important: This principle is scored based on verbal cues in the transcript only.
Since you are analysing transcripts, not video footage, you cannot see visual elements.
Score based ONLY on verbal references to visuals in the transcript.

What to look for (in transcript): Verbal references to visual elements — mentions of
charts, maps, screen shares, B-roll, walkthroughs, iPad drawings, overlays, diagrams.
Phrases like "as you can see," "look at this," "here's what that looks like,"
"let me show you," "on screen right now."

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 0–2 | No verbal references to any visual elements in transcript. |
| 3–4 | Rare verbal references to visuals (1–2 mentions across all videos). |
| 5–6 | Some verbal references but inconsistent. |
| 7–8 | Regular verbal references to visual elements throughout transcripts. |
| 9–10 | Abundant verbal cues to visuals throughout all transcripts. |

Evidence to cite: Quote specific verbal cues that reference visual elements.
Note: this is an estimate from transcript language only.
```

**2. Remove Show Don't Tell from the weighted score calculation.**

Currently the weighted score formula is:
- 3x: Lead Magnet System, Avatar Clarity, Binge Architecture
- 2x: ARC Attention, Approve the Click, Connection Language, Title Frameworks, ARC Revelation, Story Proof
- 1x: Themes Over Topics, Consistency, **Show Don't Tell**, Curiosity Bridges, Values Peppering, Grade 5 Language, ARC Connection
- Denominator: **28**

Change to:
- 3x: Lead Magnet System, Avatar Clarity, Binge Architecture (= 9)
- 2x: ARC Attention, Approve the Click, Connection Language, Title Frameworks, ARC Revelation, Story Proof (= 12)
- 1x: Themes Over Topics, Consistency, Curiosity Bridges, Values Peppering, Grade 5 Language, ARC Connection (= 6)
- **Show Don't Tell: 0x weight** (still scored, still displayed on report, just not in the calculation)
- Denominator: **27**

**3. Update Content Impact dimension** (used in lead audit reports):

Old: `Content Impact = average(ARC Attention, ARC Revelation, Approve the Click, Title Frameworks, Show Don't Tell, Curiosity Bridges)` — divided by 6

New: `Content Impact = average(ARC Attention, ARC Revelation, Approve the Click, Title Frameworks, Curiosity Bridges)` — divided by 5

**4. On the report UI**, Show Don't Tell should still appear in the 16-Principle Scorecard but with a small note or icon indicating it's transcript-estimated and not included in the overall score. Something like a small "(est.)" or tooltip.

---

## Summary of Code Changes Needed

1. **Scoring prompt text** — update Consistency rubric (mathematical calculation) and Show Don't Tell rubric (verbal cues only)
2. **Weighted score calculation** — remove Show Don't Tell from numerator, change denominator from 28 to 27
3. **Content Impact dimension** — remove Show Don't Tell, divide by 5 instead of 6
4. **Report UI** — mark Show Don't Tell as estimated/excluded from score
5. **Scoring prompt rules** — add explicit instruction to calculate Consistency mathematically
