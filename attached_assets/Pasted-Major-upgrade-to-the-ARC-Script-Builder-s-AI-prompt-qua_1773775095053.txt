Major upgrade to the ARC Script Builder's AI prompt
  quality. The current prompts produce generic scripts      
  because they don't enforce the right inputs or quality
  standards. Here are the changes:                          
                  
  === CHANGE 1: UPGRADE THE CREDIBILITY STEP ===            
   
  The current credibility input is a single textarea with a 
  vague placeholder. Replace with structured prompts that
  force specificity:                                        
                  
  Instead of one textarea, show 4 short input fields:       
  1. "How many clients/families/businesses have you helped?"
   (e.g., "200+ families")                                  
  2. "What's a specific result you've helped someone
  achieve?" (e.g., "Helped a family save $40K by timing     
  their sale")    
  3. "What's a stat about how often you do this work?"      
  (e.g., "We close a deal every 6 days")                    
  4. "What would surprise someone about your track record?"
  (e.g., "22 years in Calgary real estate, started before   
  the 2008 crash")
                                                            
  All fields optional but at least one required. These get  
  passed as structured data (not a blob) to the AI prompt
  with the instruction: "Use these credentials EXACTLY as   
  written. Do not replace with generic language like 'years
  of experience.' Use specific numbers and results."

  === CHANGE 2: ADD A REAL CLIENT STORY INPUT ===

  Add a new section to Step 3 (Build Your Insights) — a     
  "Client Story Bank" area that appears before/alongside the
   Value Loop fields:                                       
                  
  1. Show a textarea: "Share a real client story for this   
  video"
  2. Helper text: "Include a name (real or changed), a      
  specific situation, a moment where something shifted, and 
  what happened. 3-5 sentences."                 
  3. Example in placeholder: "Mark and Dana came to us after
   sitting on the fence for 2 years. Their rent had gone up 
  twice. They thought they needed 20% down. When we showed
  them the math on 5% down vs waiting, they realized waiting
   was costing them $800/month in lost equity. They closed 6
   weeks later."                                 
  4. If the member leaves it blank, the AI prompt must say:
  "The member hasn't provided a client story. In the STORY  
  section of each Value Loop, write a PLACEHOLDER that says
  '[INSERT YOUR REAL CLIENT STORY HERE — a name, a specific 
  situation, what shifted, and what happened]' — do NOT
  invent a generic story."                       

  This is critical — AI-invented stories are the #1 reason  
  scripts sound fake.
                                                            
  === CHANGE 3: DEEPEN THE VIEWER EMOTIONAL STATE INPUTS ===
                                                 
  The current "How does your viewer feel BEFORE/AFTER"      
  fields are too shallow. Replace with:
                                                            
  1. "What emotion is your viewer feeling right now?" (keep 
  this)                                          
  2. NEW: "What question are they asking themselves that    
  they won't say out loud?" — placeholder: "e.g., Did we    
  wait too long? Are we being greedy? Can we actually afford
   this?"                                                   
  3. NEW: "What are they afraid this video might confirm?" —
   placeholder: "e.g., That they missed their window, that  
  they're not ready, that this will be harder than they
  thought"                                                  
  4. NEW: "What do they secretly hope this video will tell
  them?" — placeholder: "e.g., That it's not too late, that 
  their situation is normal, that there's a clear next step"
                                                            
  These 4 fields replace the before/after textareas. Pass   
  ALL of them to every AI prompt with: "Write every insight
  speaking directly to this person's internal state — not a 
  general audience."
                                                 
  === CHANGE 4: FRAME UNIQUE ANGLE AGAINST CONVENTIONAL     
  WISDOM ===
                                                            
  The current "unique approach" textarea doesn't create     
  enough contrast. Split it into two fields:     
                                                            
  1. "What does conventional wisdom say about this topic?" —
   placeholder: "What does everyone else in your industry
  tell people about this?"                                  
  2. "What do YOU believe that's different?" — placeholder:
  "What have you seen with real clients that contradicts the
   standard advice?"
                                                            
  The AI prompt should include: "The conventional wisdom is:
   [field 1]. The member's unique angle is: [field 2]. Write
   ALL insights from the member's perspective — not the     
  industry standard view. The script should feel like the
  member is correcting a common misconception."  

  === CHANGE 5: UPGRADE THE AI SYSTEM PROMPT FOR FINAL      
  GENERATION ===
                                                            
  Add these quality enforcement rules to the "final" step   
  prompt:                                        
                                                            
  VALUES PLACEMENT:
  "Weave in the member's values naturally — do not turn them
   into a section or list them explicitly. Each value should
   appear ONCE, in passing, the way someone would say it in
  a real conversation. They should feel like personality,   
  not a mission statement."
                                                 
  CURIOSITY BRIDGES:                                        
  "For each curiosity bridge between sections, create an
  information gap the viewer needs to close. The bridge     
  should make the viewer think 'wait, what does that mean?'
  — not 'okay, moving on.' Do not write summary transitions.
   Open a door to the next section that the viewer has to
  walk through."                                 

  INSIGHT QUALITY FILTER:                                   
  "Before writing each insight, check: would a viewer say
  'I've never thought about it that way'? If the answer is  
  no — if it's something a basic Google search would return
  — rewrite it until it earns that reaction. Generic advice 
  disguised as an insight is not acceptable. Each insight
  must reveal something the viewer didn't know they didn't
  know."

  CLIENT STORIES:                                           
  "If the member provided a real client story, use it in the
   most impactful Value Loop's STORY section. Reference the 
  real name, real details, and real outcome. For other Value
   Loops, adapt elements of the story or write '[INSERT YOUR
   STORY HERE]' placeholders — never invent generic
  stories."                                      

  CREDENTIAL ENFORCEMENT:                                   
  "Use the member's credentials EXACTLY as provided. If they
   said '22 years,' write '22 years' — not '8 years' or     
  'years of experience.' The member's specific numbers are
  non-negotiable."                                          
                  
  === HOW TO TEST ===                            
  - [ ] Credibility step shows 4 structured fields instead
  of one textarea                                           
  - [ ] Client story input appears with helper text and
  placeholder example                                       
  - [ ] Leaving client story blank produces "[INSERT YOUR
  STORY]" placeholders, not invented stories                
  - [ ] Viewer emotional state has 4 fields (emotion,
  internal question, fear, hope)                            
  - [ ] Unique angle split into "conventional wisdom" vs
  "your take"                                               
  - [ ] Final script uses member's exact credentials (test:
  enter "22 years" → verify "22 years" in output)           
  - [ ] Values appear once each, naturally woven — not as a
  list                                                      
  - [ ] Curiosity bridges create information gaps, not
  summary transitions                                       
  - [ ] Each insight passes the "never thought about it that
   way" test — no generic advice
